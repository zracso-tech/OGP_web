Many product teams debate methodologies, frameworks and prioritisation. But there's one variable that conditions absolutely everything before you write the first line of the roadmap:

**Who are you selling to, and how does your product reach their hands?**

Because building product in B2B and in B2C isn't a question of scale. It's a question of completely different logic.

## How the product is built differently

In B2C you design for emotion and immediacy. The user decides in seconds. The experience has to be self-explanatory, addictive and frictionless. Every tenth of a second in onboarding matters. Every notification has to earn its place.

In B2B you design for the workflow. Nobody buys a service or product because it excites them: they buy it because it solves a need. Depth matters more than beauty.

## How Go-to-Market changes

In B2C, the service or product distributes itself or it doesn't get distributed at all. The growth loop, virality, word of mouth and paid acquisition are your levers. Nobody calls a salesperson to sign up.

In B2B, the product opens the conversation, but there's an evaluation process, a buying committee, a contract and an implementation team. The roadmap has to be aligned with what the sales team needs to close and with what Customer Success needs to retain.

## The metrics that guide decisions

In B2C you look at DAU, D7/D30 retention, CAC, LTV and conversion rate at massive scale.

In B2B you look at NRR, account expansion, time-to-value, churn by segment and product-influenced pipeline.

They're different languages. And making decisions while mixing the two without realising it is one of the most common mistakes.

## And when the model is hybrid? Towards B2B2C

More and more products have a B2C acquisition leg and a B2B monetisation leg. Or the other way around: they start selling to companies and want to reach the end user (B2B2C).

That's where the CPO's strategic clarity makes the difference: knowing which logic applies at each phase and not trying to optimise both at the same time.
