Do you think ethics is just a section on the corporate website? It's the invisible code that defines every decision a company makes.

From the CPO seat, there's something that becomes obvious over time: the product never lies.

You can invest in marketing, optimise funnels or redefine your value proposition. But if inside the organisation there's friction, misalignment or a weak or toxic culture, that always ends up reflected in the product or in the delivery of a service.

Because the product is the direct reflection of how people work inside:

- Teams that collaborate generate coherent experiences.
- Clear leadership brings consistent product decisions.
- A culture of respect conveys trust to the customer.
- Healthy environments unleash real innovation, not forced.

But there's something even more decisive. When teams work in a healthy, positive environment, they don't just work better: they work differently, they're happier, they're more focused.

- More motivation, which brings greater productivity and quality.
- More confidence, which fosters more initiative and less paralysis.
- More wellbeing, which gives a healthier, more creative vision applied to the product.
- More involvement: teams that propose, not constrained, not ones that execute without questioning.

They're not merely "aligned" teams, but connected with what they do. And that shows, because when people aren't sidelined, when they feel they can genuinely contribute, the product stops being a list of features and becomes a collective construction with intent.

And the reverse happens too: organisations with internal noise generate confusing products, companies afraid of error launch mediocre products, and worn-out cultures build experiences the customer perceives… even if they can't explain it.

The user doesn't see your culture, but they feel it: in the usability, in the speed of response, in the quality of every interaction. That's why building product isn't just about prioritising features or managing a roadmap. It's, to a large extent, about designing the environment where that product is born.

If you're a company with a good atmosphere, where people trust, contribute and feel part of something, that comes across. And the market notices. Not always immediately, but in a sustained way. Because great products aren't only well built: they're well "lived" from within.

👉 Does your product reflect the culture you claim to have inside your organisation?
